Welcome

Bioinformatics is an applied interdisciplinary field whose primary purpose is to develop and deploy computational techniques to store, organize, and aid in the analysis and interpretation of large-scale data obtained from biological systems. While rooted in the analysis of nucleotide and protein sequences, it now encompasses techniques targeting multiple data acquisition modalities and seeks to comprehend the functioning of biological systems at many different levels. Bioinformaticians need to be cognizant of diverse scientific fields: basic and molecular biology, medecine, genomics, computational sciences, statistics, and computational medicine, thus requiring a thoroughly interdisciplinary set of skills to successfully apply bioinformatics methods on large scale biomedical datasets.

Despite the growing importance of bioinformatics and computational medicine in enabling modern computational data-driven biomedical research, Moldova has not yet developed or established bioinformatics programs and core facilities in the academic institutions. To fill this gap, the body formed by the Technical University of Moldova (TUM), the Nicolae Testemitanu State University of Medicine and Pharmacy (SUMP), and the Moldova State University (MSU) in collaboration with Mangul Lab at University of Southern California School of Pharmacy (Los Angeles, USA), with funding support from United States Agency for International Development (USAID), proposes the creation of an Eastern European bioinformatics knowledge hub to develop and promote bioinformatics knowledge and skills in the field of computational medicine and biomedical data science.

We propose to create a bioinformatics school through which we will provide an introduction to the use of bioinformatics approaches in biomedical research, giving participants guidance for using bioinformatics in their work. We also will provide hands-on training in tools and resources appropriate to their research.Participants will initially be introduced to bioinformatics theory and practice, including best practices for undertaking bioinformatics analysis, data management, version control systems and reproducibility. The objective of this course is to train students in the state of the art bioinformatics tools and methods for rigorous, and reproducible biomedical data analysis, including methods to visualize display data and perform appropriate statistical analysis. Students are expected to learn how to:

  • Analyze the large-scale biomedical data using commonly used interactive python-based libraries and packages
  • Biomedical databases and data integration
  • Biomedical text mining and ortologies
  • Biomolecular imaging
  • Comparative genomics
  • Computational genetic epidemiology
  • Computational proteomics
  • Data mining and visualization
  • Gene expression analysis
  • Structural biology
  • Genome analysis
  • Systems biology
  • High-performance bio-computing
  • Metagenomics
  • Molecular evolution
  • Molecular modelling and simulation
  • Next-generation sequencing data analysis
  • Pattern discovery and classification
  • Population genetics
  • Software tools and applications
  • AI and machine learning methods in Bioinformatics and Medical Information
  • Big Data Analytics in Biology and Medicine
